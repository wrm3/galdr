# Benefits Administration

Benefits packages by company stage, 401(k) options, PTO policies, and cost estimates.

## Benefits Package by Stage

### Seed (1-10 employees)

```
Essential:
- Health insurance (via PEO like Gusto, Justworks, or Rippling)
- Unlimited PTO (or generous fixed PTO)
- Remote work flexibility

Estimated cost: ~$500-800/employee/month
```

### Series A (10-30 employees)

```
Add:
- Dental & Vision insurance
- 401(k) (no match initially)
- Life insurance
- Parental leave (8-12 weeks)

Estimated cost: ~$800-1,200/employee/month
```

### Series B+ (30+ employees)

```
Add:
- 401(k) match (3-4%)
- Extended parental leave (16+ weeks)
- Professional development budget ($1,000-5,000/yr)
- Wellness stipend ($50-150/mo)
- Commuter benefits

Estimated cost: ~$1,200-1,800/employee/month
```

## 401(k) Plan Options

| Type | Match | Vesting | Testing Required |
|------|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Traditional | Discretionary | Up to 6 years | Yes |
| Safe Harbor | 3-4% required | Immediate | No |
| SIMPLE | 3% or 2% non-elective | Immediate | No |

**Safe Harbor** is recommended for startups — avoids non-discrimination testing and provides immediate vesting, which is attractive for recruiting.

## Health Insurance Approaches

| Approach | Best For | Pros | Cons |
|----------|----------|------|------|
| PEO (Gusto, Justworks) | <50 employees | Easy setup, pooled rates | Less plan customization |
| Broker + carrier | 20-100 employees | More plan options | More admin overhead |
| Self-insured | 100+ employees | Cost control, data access | Financial risk, complexity |
| ICHRA | Mixed workforce | Per-employee budgets | Employees shop individually |

## PTO Policies

### Unlimited PTO
- **Pros**: Simple to administer, no accrual liability
- **Cons**: Employees may take less time off; no payout on termination
- **Best practice**: Set minimum PTO (e.g., "take at least 15 days/year")

### Fixed PTO
- **Typical**: 15-20 days/year + holidays + sick time
- **Accrual**: Usually accrues per pay period
- **Payout**: Required in some states (CA, CO, IL, etc.) on termination

## Parental Leave Benchmarks

| Stage | Birthing Parent | Non-Birthing Parent |
|-------|----------------|---------------------|
| Seed | 8-12 weeks | 4-6 weeks |
| Series A | 12-16 weeks | 6-8 weeks |
| Series B+ | 16-20 weeks | 8-12 weeks |

Note: FMLA provides 12 weeks unpaid leave for companies with 50+ employees. State laws (CA, NY, WA, etc.) may provide paid leave.

## Benefits Compliance

- **ACA**: Companies with 50+ FTEs must offer affordable health coverage
- **COBRA**: Companies with 20+ employees must offer continuation coverage
- **ERISA**: Governs 401(k) and health plans; requires plan documents and fiduciary duty
- **State mandates**: Some states require disability insurance, paid family leave, or specific benefits
