# Investor Outreach

VC research workflow, warm introduction strategy, cold outreach templates, and pipeline tracking.

## Building Your VC Target List

### Step 1: Define Criteria
- **Stage**: Pre-seed, seed, Series A, B, C+
- **Check size**: Minimum and maximum investment
- **Industry focus**: SaaS, fintech, biotech, consumer, etc.
- **Geography**: Preferred location or remote-friendly
- **Thesis alignment**: Specific trends or technologies

### Step 2: Research Sources
| Source | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Crunchbase ($29-99/mo) | Search VCs by criteria, recent investments |
| AngelList (free) | VC profiles, portfolio companies, thesis |
| PitchBook ($7,000+/yr) | Detailed VC data, fund performance |
| LinkedIn (free/$40/mo) | Partner profiles, backgrounds, mutual connections |
| Twitter/X (free) | VC activity, thought leadership, interests |
| VC websites | Investment criteria, portfolio, team bios |
| SignalFire Beacon (free) | VC database |

### Step 3: Prioritize into Tiers
| Tier | Criteria | Action |
|------|----------|--------|
| Tier 1 | Perfect fit (thesis, stage, check size, geography) | Pursue warm intros first |
| Tier 2 | Good fit (most criteria match) | Mix of warm + cold |
| Tier 3 | Possible fit (some criteria match) | Cold outreach |
| Tier 4 | Stretch (backup options) | Only if needed |

### Step 4: Research Each VC
For every Tier 1-2 firm, document:
- Portfolio companies (especially competitors or adjacent)
- Recent investments (is the fund actively deploying?)
- Partner backgrounds and interests
- Investment thesis and focus areas
- Fund size and investment pace
- Follow-on investment strategy

### Target List Size
| Round | Target VCs |
|-------|-----------|
| Pre-Seed | 50-80 |
| Seed | 100-150 |
| Series A | 75-100 |
| Series B+ | 50-75 |

## Warm Introduction Strategy

### Priority Order (Most to Least Effective)
1. Portfolio founder intros
2. Mutual investor intros
3. Advisor/mentor intros
4. Accelerator/incubator intros
5. Conference/event connections
6. LinkedIn mutual connections

### Warm Intro Process
1. Identify your connection to the target VC partner
2. Prepare a 3-4 sentence intro blurb
3. Ask the connector if they'd genuinely recommend you
4. Provide context on what you need
5. Send the forwardable blurb to the connector
6. Follow up once the intro is made
7. Thank the connector after the meeting

### Intro Request Template
```
Subject: Quick favor — intro to [Partner Name] at [VC Firm]?

Hi [Connector],

Hope you're doing well! We're raising our [stage] round and
[VC Firm] is a strong fit for what we're building.

I noticed you know [Partner Name]. Would you feel comfortable
making an introduction? We're [one sentence about company]
and have [key traction metric].

Here's a blurb you could forward:

"[Partner], I wanted to introduce you to [Founder], CEO of
[Company]. They're building [one sentence] and have [traction].
They're raising [amount] and I thought they'd fit [VC Firm]'s
thesis around [relevant area]."

No worries if timing isn't right — just thought I'd ask!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Cold Outreach Strategy

### Email Best Practices
- Personalize every email (no mass sends)
- Keep it to 5-7 sentences
- Lead with traction or a unique insight
- Explain why THIS VC specifically
- Clear call to action (15-min call)
- Optionally attach one-pager (not full deck)

### Cold Email Template
```
Subject: [Company] — [one sentence pitch]

Hi [Partner First Name],

I'm [Name], founder of [Company] — [one sentence description].
We're [key traction metric] and raising [$XM stage].

I'm reaching out because [VC Firm] is a strong fit:
• [Reason 1: thesis alignment or portfolio company]
• [Reason 2: partner expertise or geographic focus]

We've built [key differentiator] and are seeing [growth metric].
[Customer validation: "Company X said..." or case study].

Would you be open to a 15-min call? Happy to send our deck.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Title] | [Contact info] | [LinkedIn]

P.S. [Reference a recent VC investment, tweet, or article]
```

### Follow-Up Cadence
| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 4-5 | "Bumping this up — any interest?" |
| Day 10-12 | Share a new traction metric or milestone |
| Day 20-25 | Final follow-up with updated materials |
| After 3 emails | Move on; revisit in 3-6 months |

### Other Outreach Channels
- LinkedIn message (if no email response)
- Twitter/X DM (if the partner is active there)
- VC firm application form
- In-person at conferences or events

## Conference & Event Networking

### Before the Event
- Research attending VCs (event list, LinkedIn)
- Prepare a 30-second elevator pitch
- Set a goal: 5-10 VC conversations
- Have digital contact sharing ready

### At the Event
- Attend VC panels and sessions
- Ask thoughtful questions publicly
- Approach VCs during breaks — be genuine, not salesy
- Collect contact info and take notes after each conversation

### Event Follow-Up Template
```
Subject: Great meeting you at [Event]

Hi [Partner Name],

Great chatting at [Event] about [specific topic].

As mentioned, I'm building [Company] — [one sentence].
We're [key metric] and raising [$XM].

Would you be open to a quick call next week?
I've attached our deck for context.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

Send within 24-48 hours of the event.

## Pipeline Tracking

### Spreadsheet Columns
1. VC Firm Name
2. Partner Name (primary contact)
3. Stage Focus
4. Check Size ($XXk-$XXM)
5. Industry Focus
6. Tier (1-4)
7. Warm Intro Path
8. Outreach Status (not contacted → reached out → meeting scheduled → passed/advancing)
9. Last Contact Date
10. Next Action
11. Notes

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up

### Within 24 Hours
- Send thank-you email
- Recap key discussion points
- Provide any requested materials
- Share customer reference contacts
- Clarify next steps and timeline

### Follow-Up Email Template
```
Subject: Thanks for the meeting — [Company]

Hi [Partner Name],

Thanks for meeting today. I enjoyed discussing [specific topic]
and your insights on [something they mentioned].

As discussed, here are the materials you requested:
• [Material 1]
• [Material 2]

I'll connect you with [Customer Name] at [Company] who can
speak to [specific use case/results].

Looking forward to [next step].

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Ongoing Cadence
- Week 1: Check in on timeline
- Week 2: Share a product update or new customer win
- Week 3: Provide requested diligence materials
- Week 4+: Monthly updates if the process is ongoing
