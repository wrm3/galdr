# Tax & Compliance

Tax calendar, federal/state compliance requirements, and Delaware corporation obligations.

## US Tax Calendar

| Date | Filing | Who |
|------|--------|-----|
| Jan 15 | Q4 estimated taxes | All entities |
| Jan 31 | W-2s to employees | All employers |
| Jan 31 | 1099-NEC to contractors | If paid >$600 |
| Mar 1 | Delaware Franchise Tax | DE corporations |
| Mar 15 | S-Corp / Partnership returns (or extension) | S-Corps, LLCs |
| Apr 15 | C-Corp returns (or extension) | C-Corporations |
| Apr 15 | Q1 estimated taxes | All entities |
| Jun 15 | Q2 estimated taxes | All entities |
| Sep 15 | Q3 estimated taxes | All entities |
| Sep 15 | Extended S-Corp / Partnership returns | If extension filed |
| Oct 15 | Extended C-Corp returns | If extension filed |

## Federal Compliance (All US Companies)

```
□ EIN obtained from IRS
□ I-9 verification for all employees (within 3 days of hire)
□ W-4 collected from all employees
□ Quarterly Form 941 payroll tax filings
□ Annual W-2s (to employees by Jan 31)
□ Annual 1099-NEC (to contractors by Jan 31)
□ OSHA compliance (if applicable)
□ FLSA compliance (overtime, minimum wage)
□ ACA reporting (if 50+ FTEs)
```

## State Compliance (Varies by State)

```
□ State tax registration (in each state with employees/nexus)
□ State unemployment insurance (SUI)
□ Workers' compensation insurance
□ State-specific leave laws (CA, NY, WA, etc.)
□ Pay transparency requirements (CA, CO, NY, WA)
□ Harassment training (CA, NY, IL, CT, DE, ME)
□ State income tax withholding
□ Sales tax registration and filing (if applicable)
```

## Delaware Corporation Requirements

```
□ Annual Franchise Tax (due March 1)
  - Minimum: $400/year
  - Use Authorized Shares method for startups (usually cheaper)
□ Annual Report filed with Division of Corporations
□ Registered agent maintained in Delaware
□ Stock ledger maintained and current
□ Board meeting minutes documented
□ Written consents for board/shareholder actions
```

## Estimated Tax Payments

```
Quarterly estimated taxes apply when you expect to owe $1,000+

Safe harbor: Pay 100% of prior year tax (110% if AGI > $150K)
             OR 90% of current year tax

Underpayment penalty: ~8% annualized (varies by year)
```

## Multi-State Considerations

- **Nexus**: Having employees in a state generally creates tax nexus
- **Remote employees**: Register for payroll tax in each state where employees work
- **Sales tax**: Economic nexus thresholds vary ($100K revenue or 200 transactions in most states)
- **Franchise tax**: Some states (TX, CA, DE) have separate franchise/gross receipts taxes
