# Term Sheet Guide

Components of a venture capital term sheet, negotiation priorities, and red flags to watch for.

## Key Term Sheet Sections

### 1. Valuation and Investment
- **Pre-money valuation**: Company value before investment
- **Post-money valuation**: Pre-money + investment amount
- **Investment amount**: Total dollars being invested
- **Price per share**: Investment amount ÷ new shares issued

**Example**:
- Pre-money: $8M, Investment: $2M → Post-money: $10M
- New investor ownership: 20%

### 2. Liquidation Preference
| Type | How It Works | Recommendation |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| 1x Non-Participating | Investor gets 1x back OR converts to common (whichever is higher) | **STANDARD — accept** |
| 1x Participating | Investor gets 1x back AND shares remaining with common | Avoid if possible |
| 2x+ Multiple | Investor gets 2x+ investment back before common sees anything | **RED FLAG — reject** |

**Exit scenario** ($10M exit, $2M invested at $8M pre, 20% ownership):
- 1x Non-Participating: Investor gets max($2M, 20% × $10M) = $2M
- 1x Participating: Investor gets $2M + 20% × $8M = $3.6M
- Founders receive $8M vs $6.4M — a $1.6M difference

### 3. Anti-Dilution Protection
| Type | Effect | Recommendation |
|------|--------|----------------|
| Broad-based weighted average | Adjusts price using all outstanding shares | **STANDARD — accept** |
| Narrow-based weighted average | Adjusts using only preferred shares (more protective for investor) | Push back |
| Full ratchet | Reprices ALL investor shares to the new lower price | **RED FLAG — reject** |

### 4. Board Composition
- **Seed**: Often no formal board seats for investors (board observer instead)
- **Series A**: Typically 1 VC seat (3-person board: 2 founders + 1 VC)
- **Series B+**: May be 2 VC seats (5-person board: 2 founders + 2 VCs + 1 independent)
- Founders should maintain board control through Series A at minimum

### 5. Voting Rights & Protective Provisions
Investors typically require approval for:
- Sale or liquidation of the company
- Issuing new equity or debt above a threshold
- Changing the certificate of incorporation
- Declaring dividends
- Increasing option pool beyond agreed size

Limit protective provisions to genuinely major events only.

### 6. Pro-Rata Rights
- Right to invest in future rounds to maintain ownership percentage
- Standard for lead investors; often extended to others with conditions
- **Super pro-rata** (right to invest MORE than pro-rata) — negotiate carefully

### 7. Drag-Along Rights
- Majority shareholders can force minority to sell in an acquisition
- Standard with conditions: minimum price threshold, board approval
- Ensure fair process protections

### 8. No-Shop / Exclusivity Period
| Duration | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| 30 days | Reasonable |
| 45 days | Acceptable |
| 60+ days | **RED FLAG** — too long |

### 9. Closing Conditions
Standard: due diligence completion, legal documentation, board/stockholder approval.
Watch for unusual conditions that could kill the deal.

## Negotiation Priority Order

1. **Partner and firm fit** — cannot be changed after signing
2. **Board composition and control** — critical for governance
3. **Liquidation preference** — insist on 1x non-participating
4. **Anti-dilution** — broad-based weighted average only
5. **Valuation** — important but not everything
6. **Pro-rata rights** — standard for lead investor
7. **Voting rights** — reasonable protective provisions only

## Red Flags (Push Back Hard)

- Multiple liquidation preference (2x+)
- Participating preferred
- Full ratchet anti-dilution
- Excessive board control by investors
- Super voting rights
- No-shop period > 60 days
- Unusual closing conditions

## Comparing Multiple Term Sheets

Weight factors when choosing between offers:

| Category | Weight | What to Compare |
|----------|--------|-----------------|
| Partner & Firm | 40% | Expertise, reputation, portfolio references, value-add, follow-on capital |
| Economic Terms | 30% | Valuation, liquidation preference, anti-dilution, option pool, dilution |
| Control Terms | 30% | Board seats, voting rights, drag-along, founder vesting, information rights |

## Negotiation Tactics

- Have multiple term sheets if possible (creates leverage)
- Consult an experienced startup lawyer before responding
- Focus on win-win outcomes — you're choosing a long-term partner
- Don't over-negotiate minor points (signals difficulty to work with)
- Be transparent about concerns
- Seek advice from founders who've worked with the VC
- Be prepared to walk away if terms are truly unfavorable
