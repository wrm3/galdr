# Due Diligence Preparation

Data room organization, due diligence checklist, customer reference management, and timeline expectations.

## Data Room Folder Structure

Organize your data room into these standard folders before sharing with investors.

### Company Overview
- Certificate of incorporation
- Bylaws
- Board resolutions and consents
- Stockholder agreements
- Capitalization table and stock ledger
- Previous fundraising documents (SAFEs, notes, prior rounds)

### Financial Information
- Financial statements (last 3 years, or since founding)
- Monthly P&L, balance sheet, cash flow
- Revenue by customer and product line
- AR aging and collections data
- Budget and forecast model (3-5 year)
- Cap table and option pool details
- Bank statements (last 12 months)

### Contracts and Agreements
- Customer contracts (top 20 by revenue)
- Vendor and supplier agreements
- Partnership agreements
- Lease agreements
- Employment agreements (founders + key hires)
- Contractor agreements
- Loan documents and credit facilities

### Intellectual Property
- Patent applications and grants
- Trademark registrations
- Copyright registrations
- IP assignment agreements (all employees/contractors)
- Licenses (inbound and outbound)
- Open source software usage and compliance
- Domain name registrations

### Legal and Compliance
- Material litigation and disputes
- Regulatory filings and compliance records
- Privacy policy and data protection documentation (GDPR, CCPA)
- Insurance policies (D&O, E&O, general liability)
- Tax returns and filings (federal and state)
- Permits and licenses

### Product and Technology
- Technical architecture diagrams
- Security documentation and audit results
- Infrastructure and hosting details
- Development roadmap
- Bug and issue tracking summary
- Customer support metrics (response time, CSAT)
- Product analytics dashboard

### Sales and Marketing
- Sales pipeline and funnel metrics
- Marketing materials and campaign performance
- Customer acquisition cost breakdown by channel
- Pricing and packaging documentation
- Customer case studies (3-5)
- Sales deck and collateral

### HR and Organization
- Organization chart
- Employee roster with titles and start dates
- Compensation structure and salary bands
- Benefits and equity plans
- Employee handbook
- Key hire performance summaries
- Hiring plan for the next 12-18 months

## Due Diligence Checklist

### Week 1-2: Initial Review
- [ ] Share data room access with VC (DocSend, Google Drive, or Dropbox)
- [ ] Provide initial document set (all folders populated)
- [ ] Schedule kickoff call with VC diligence team
- [ ] Assign internal point person for DD requests
- [ ] Set up communication channel (email thread or Slack)
- [ ] Agree on DD timeline and milestones

### Week 2-3: Document Review
- [ ] Respond to document requests within 24-48 hours
- [ ] Provide additional contracts and agreements as requested
- [ ] Walk through financial model with VC
- [ ] Conduct technical architecture review session
- [ ] Facilitate legal document review
- [ ] Address initial questions and concerns

### Week 3-4: Deep Dives
- [ ] Arrange customer reference calls (3-5 customers)
- [ ] Conduct product demo and technical walkthrough
- [ ] Schedule management team interviews
- [ ] Provide market and competitive analysis data
- [ ] Support financial audit if required
- [ ] Facilitate legal compliance review

### Week 4-6: Final Items
- [ ] Address all remaining concerns and questions
- [ ] Provide third-party reports (credit checks, background checks)
- [ ] Complete final financial review
- [ ] Begin legal documentation preparation
- [ ] Obtain board and stockholder approvals
- [ ] Coordinate closing logistics and wire instructions

## Customer References

### Selecting References
Choose 3-5 customers that represent:
- Different company sizes (SMB, mid-market, enterprise)
- Different industries or use cases
- Strong product adoption and measurable results
- Willingness to speak candidly with investors
- Ideally recognizable company names

### Preparing References
Before connecting them with the VC:
1. Brief them on which VC and the context of the conversation
2. Share 3-4 discussion topics the VC will likely ask about
3. Remind them of specific successes and ROI numbers
4. Ask them to be honest — VCs respect candor
5. Thank them in advance and follow up after

### Common Reference Topics
- Why they chose your product over alternatives
- Implementation and onboarding experience
- Measurable results and ROI
- Product strengths and areas for improvement
- Quality of support and customer success
- Plans for expanding usage
- Whether they'd recommend you to peers

## Managing the DD Process

### Best Practices
- Respond to every request within 24-48 hours
- Over-communicate: send weekly status updates
- Be transparent about challenges — VCs will find them anyway
- Provide context for any concerns (explain, don't hide)
- Keep the process moving forward with clear next steps
- Maintain urgency without being pushy

### Common Issues and Solutions
| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Slow VC response | Follow up directly, escalate to the partner |
| Extensive document requests | Ask what's critical path vs nice-to-have |
| Concern or red flag raised | Address head-on with data and transparency |
| Timeline slipping | Reset expectations, create urgency with competing interest |
| Multiple VCs in DD simultaneously | Coordinate to avoid duplicating effort |
