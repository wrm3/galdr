# Business Operations Skill

HR, compensation & benefits, and accounting/finance operations for startups and growing companies.

## Important Disclaimer

**This skill provides educational information only, not legal, tax, or financial advice.**
- Consult employment attorneys for HR legal matters
- Consult CPAs for tax and accounting matters
- Consult benefits brokers for benefits design
- Laws vary by state/jurisdiction and change frequently

## When to Use

- Planning a hire: job descriptions, interview process, offer letters
- Designing compensation packages: salary bands, equity grants, vesting schedules
- Setting up or reviewing benefits: health insurance, 401(k), PTO policies
- Bookkeeping and financial reporting: chart of accounts, monthly close, financial statements
- Tax compliance: filing deadlines, estimated payments, W-2/1099 obligations
- Employee classification: W-2 vs 1099, misclassification risk

## Process

1. **Identify the Area** — Determine whether the question involves HR, compensation, benefits, finance, or compliance. Route to the appropriate reference file.
2. **Check Compliance** — Review `reference/tax_compliance.md` for deadlines and regulatory requirements before making decisions.
3. **Apply Templates** — Use the templates in `reference/hiring_process.md` for JDs, scorecards, and onboarding checklists.
4. **Model Costs** — Use `reference/compensation_equity.md` and `reference/benefits_admin.md` to estimate total compensation and benefits costs.
5. **Record & Report** — Follow `reference/bookkeeping_finance.md` for proper accounting treatment and financial statement preparation.

## Employee Classification Quick Reference

| Type | Characteristics | Tax Forms |
|------|-----------------|-----------|
| W-2 Employee | Set hours, company equipment, ongoing | W-4, I-9, W-2 |
| 1099 Contractor | Own schedule, own tools, project-based | W-9, 1099-NEC |

**Misclassification risk factors**: behavioral control, financial control, relationship type.
